Accessibility Features We Install

Our accessibility remodeling services include a comprehensive range of modifications designed to reduce fall risk, improve mobility, and increase independence:

  • Walk-in showers — Barrier-free, curbless shower designs with zero-threshold entry for easy wheelchair or walker access.
  • Grab bars and safety rails — Professionally anchored grab bars at the toilet, shower, and bathtub in ADA-compliant positions for maximum support.
  • Comfort-height toilets — ADA-height toilets that sit 17 to 19 inches from the floor, making it easier to sit down and stand up.
  • Built-in shower bench seats — Tiled bench seats and fold-down shower chairs for safe, comfortable seated bathing.
  • Handheld shower heads — Adjustable-height shower fixtures that allow bathing from a seated or standing position.
  • Non-slip tile flooring — Textured, anti-slip tile installation on floors and shower surfaces to reduce fall risk in wet conditions.
  • Wider doorways — Expanding bathroom doorways to 32 inches or wider to accommodate wheelchairs and walkers.
  • Lever-handle faucets — Easy-to-operate lever handles on faucets and shower controls that require minimal grip strength.
  • Accessible vanities — Open-base vanity designs with knee clearance for seated use and wheelchair access.

Aging-in-Place Remodeling

Many Edmond homeowners choose accessibility modifications not because of an immediate need, but as a smart investment in their home's long-term livability. Aging-in-place remodeling allows you to stay in the home you love rather than facing an expensive and disruptive move to an assisted living facility. A well-planned accessible bathroom can serve your household for decades, adapting to changing mobility needs while maintaining the modern design and comfort you expect.

Our aging-in-place projects often combine accessibility features with aesthetic upgrades, such as replacing an old tub with a beautiful curbless tile shower, upgrading to a modern vanity with open-base accessibility, and installing heated floors for comfort. The result is a bathroom that looks like a high-end remodel while providing the safety features your family needs.
