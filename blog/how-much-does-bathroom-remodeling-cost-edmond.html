How Much Does Bathroom Remodeling Cost in Edmond? (2026 Guide)

Updated March 2026 · Scissortail Bath · 6 min read

If you're planning a bathroom remodel in Edmond, Oklahoma, the first question on your mind is almost certainly: how much is this going to cost? It's a fair question, and the answer depends on the scope of the project, the materials you choose, and the current condition of your bathroom. Edmond is a growing OKC suburb with above-average household incomes and a housing stock that ranges from 1980s builder-grade ranches to newer construction in neighborhoods like Deer Creek and Coffee Creek. That mix means remodeling costs here reflect a wide range of starting points.

This guide breaks down real 2026 pricing for bathroom remodels in the Edmond area, explains what drives costs up or down, and gives you the information you need to budget accurately before calling a contractor.

Average Bathroom Remodeling Costs in Edmond (2026)

Here are the typical cost ranges for bathroom remodels in the Edmond and OKC metro area, based on 2026 pricing for labor and materials:

Remodel Type Cost Range What's Included
Cosmetic Refresh $3,000 – $8,000 New fixtures, vanity swap, paint, lighting, mirror. No tile or layout changes.
Mid-Range Remodel $8,000 – $18,000 New tile, vanity, fixtures, lighting, ventilation. Same footprint and layout.
Full Gut Remodel $18,000 – $40,000+ Complete demo, layout changes, moved plumbing, custom tile, luxury fixtures, expanded footprint.

These ranges reflect Edmond-specific labor rates and material costs. The OKC metro is generally more affordable than coastal markets, but Edmond's above-average incomes and newer housing stock mean homeowners here tend to choose mid-range to high-end finishes rather than the cheapest available options.

What Factors Affect Your Bathroom Remodel Cost?

No two bathrooms are the same, and costs vary based on several key factors:

Bathroom size. A standard hall bathroom (5x8 feet) costs significantly less to remodel than a primary bathroom with a separate shower and tub, double vanity, and more square footage of tile. Expect primary bath remodels in Edmond to run 30-50% higher than secondary bathrooms of the same finish level.

Tile selection and coverage. Tile is where budgets diverge most. Basic ceramic tile installed on floors runs $5-$10 per square foot installed. Large-format porcelain, natural stone, or floor-to-ceiling tile work can push that to $15-$30+ per square foot. A shower with full tile walls, a tile niche, and a custom tile floor adds $2,000-$6,000 to the project depending on material choice.

Plumbing changes. Keeping the existing plumbing layout is the single biggest cost saver. Moving a toilet, shower, or sink to a new location requires re-routing drain and supply lines, which adds $1,500-$4,000+ depending on the complexity. Many Edmond homes built in the 1980s and 1990s have galvanized supply lines or cast iron drains that should be replaced during a remodel while the walls are open.

Fixtures and finishes. A basic toilet runs $200-$400; a comfort-height, soft-close model is $350-$700. A standard vanity with a cultured marble top is $400-$900; a custom vanity with a quartz countertop runs $1,200-$3,500. These choices add up fast and are the primary difference between the low and high ends of each cost tier.

Structural surprises. Older Edmond homes may have moisture damage behind tile, deteriorated subfloors, or outdated wiring. Oklahoma's humidity and freeze-thaw cycles accelerate moisture damage, particularly in bathrooms with inadequate ventilation. A good contractor will assess the probability of hidden damage during the consultation and recommend budgeting a 10-15% contingency.

What Should Be Included in Your Bathroom Remodel Quote?

A complete, professional bathroom remodel quote in Edmond should itemize the following:

  • Demolition and haul-away of existing materials
  • Plumbing rough-in and fixture installation
  • Electrical work (lighting, outlets, exhaust fan)
  • Waterproofing and moisture barrier (critical in Oklahoma's climate)
  • Tile installation (floors, shower walls, backsplash)
  • Vanity, countertop, and sink installation
  • Toilet replacement or reinstallation
  • Paint and trim work
  • Final cleanup and walkthrough
  • City of Edmond permits (if plumbing or electrical modifications are involved)

If a quote is missing any of these items, ask about them. Incomplete quotes are the number one reason remodel costs "balloon" beyond the original estimate. A fixed-price, line-item quote protects both you and the contractor.

How to Choose a Bathroom Remodeling Contractor in Edmond

Not all contractors are the same, and the OKC metro has both excellent remodelers and operators who cut corners. Here's what to look for:

Licensed and insured. Oklahoma requires contractors to carry general liability insurance and workers' compensation. Ask for proof before signing anything. The City of Edmond also requires building permits for plumbing and electrical work -- your contractor should pull these for you.

Written, fixed-price quotes. Avoid contractors who give verbal estimates or vague ranges. A professional remodeler will measure your space, discuss your goals, and provide a detailed written quote with every line item specified. The only legitimate additions should be hidden structural issues discovered during demolition or scope changes you request.

Portfolio and references. Ask to see completed work in the Edmond area. Tile quality is especially revealing -- look for flush edges (no lippage), consistent grout lines, and clean corners. These details separate skilled tile installers from general handymen.

Communication style. The contractor who is responsive during the sales process will be responsive during the project. If they take a week to return your call before you've signed, expect worse during the build. Daily progress updates should be standard, not a premium feature.

Tornado season scheduling. This is specific to Oklahoma: spring and early summer are peak tornado season. Experienced Edmond contractors account for weather delays in their schedules. If a contractor promises a two-week timeline starting in April without mentioning weather contingency, they haven't done enough work in this area.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of a bathroom remodel in Edmond, OK?
Bathroom remodel costs in Edmond range from $3,000 to $40,000+ depending on scope. A cosmetic refresh (new fixtures, vanity, paint) runs $3,000-$8,000. A mid-range remodel with new tile, vanity, and updated plumbing fixtures costs $8,000-$18,000. A full gut remodel with layout changes and luxury finishes ranges from $18,000 to $40,000+.
Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel in Edmond?
The City of Edmond requires building permits for bathroom remodels that involve plumbing modifications, electrical changes, or structural work. Cosmetic updates like painting, replacing a vanity, or swapping fixtures generally do not require a permit. A licensed contractor should handle the permit process on your behalf.
How long does a bathroom remodel take in Edmond?
Most bathroom remodels in Edmond take 2-4 weeks. A cosmetic refresh can be completed in 1-2 weeks. Full gut remodels with layout changes and custom tile work typically take 4-6 weeks, depending on City of Edmond permit timelines, material lead times, and weather considerations during tornado season.
Does a bathroom remodel increase home value in Edmond?
Yes. According to the Remodeling Cost vs. Value Report, bathroom remodels in the South-Central region return 60-70% of their cost at resale. In Edmond's housing market, updated bathrooms are consistently one of the top factors buyers evaluate. Outdated bathrooms with original 1980s-1990s finishes actively reduce offers.
What is the most expensive part of a bathroom remodel?
Labor and tile work are typically the largest expense, accounting for 40-50% of the total budget. Custom tile showers with floor-to-ceiling coverage are particularly labor-intensive. After tile, plumbing changes (moving drains or supply lines), custom cabinetry, and high-end fixtures are the next biggest cost drivers.
